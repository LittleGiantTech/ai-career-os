# CLAUDE.md

## Project Name

AI Engineer OS

---

## Mission

AI Engineer OS is not a learning management system, productivity app, or job readiness platform.

AI Engineer OS exists to transform Travis into a production-capable AI Product Engineer who can independently design, build, explain, defend, debug, and improve AI systems.

The goal is technical independence.

---

## Success Definition

The project succeeds when Travis can:

* Design AI architectures without AI assistance
* Build production AI systems
* Explain architectural decisions
* Defend technical tradeoffs
* Teach concepts to another learner
* Pass AI technical interviews with 90% understanding

The goal is not course completion.

The goal is mastery.

---

## Core Philosophy

Learning alone is insufficient.

Building alone is insufficient.

Completion alone is insufficient.

Every stage must follow:

Learn → Build → Explain → Defend → Improve

If a concept cannot be explained, it is not yet mastered.

---

## Product Principles

1. Optimize for mastery, not completion.
2. Optimize for understanding, not memorization.
3. Optimize for capability, not credentials.
4. Optimize for engineering judgment, not tutorials.
5. Every feature must increase the probability that Travis becomes a better AI Engineer.

If a feature does not directly contribute to this goal, it belongs in the Parking Lot.

---

## Scope Freeze

Current User Count: 1

Current User: Travis

Current Career Path Count: 1

Current Career Path: AI Product Engineer

Do not design for multiple users, multiple career paths, subscriptions, admin dashboards, enterprise features, or curriculum management.

These ideas belong in the Parking Lot until Travis becomes a production-capable AI Engineer.

---

## Curriculum Structure

The curriculum is a static TypeScript definition.

The curriculum is the source of truth.

The database stores progress only.

The curriculum stores:

* Stages
* Resources
* Concepts
* Tasks
* Challenges
* Projects
* Interview blueprints
* Reflection questions

---

## Mastery System

Stage completion requires:

1. Project completion
2. Challenge completion
3. AI interview completion
4. Minimum mastery score of 85%

Mastery is more important than completion.

---

## AI Interview System

Every stage contains an interview blueprint.

The AI interviewer must:

* Ask concept questions
* Ask application questions
* Ask architecture questions
* Ask defense questions
* Ask connection questions

Questions must align with the exact concepts taught in the stage.

The AI interviewer must remember previous weaknesses and periodically re-test them.

The goal is long-term retention.

---

## Frontend Design Principles
Use Front End Desgin Skill

The product should feel like a premium engineering operating system.

It should not feel like:

* School
* Coursera
* Udemy
* Khan Academy
* Traditional LMS software

It should feel closer to:

* Linear
* Vercel
* Raycast
* Warp
* Professional engineering tools

Design characteristics:

* Dark theme
* Orange accent color
* Sparse layouts
* Strong typography
* Minimal visual noise
* High information density
* Premium feeling
* Mission control aesthetic

Every screen should communicate:

"I am becoming an elite AI Engineer."

---

## Dashboard Purpose

The dashboard exists to fight shiny object syndrome.

The dashboard must answer:

1. What am I working on?
2. Why am I working on it?
3. What should I do next?
4. What am I intentionally not working on?
5. How much progress have I made?

---

## Decision Framework

When making product decisions:

Ask:

"Will this increase Travis's probability of becoming a production-capable AI Engineer?"

If yes:
Proceed.

If no:
Move it to the Parking Lot.

---

## Architecture Philosophy

Prefer simplicity over flexibility.

Prefer static curriculum definitions over dynamic systems.

Prefer maintainability over abstraction.

Prefer solving today's problem over future-proofing imaginary problems.

Do not introduce complexity for hypothetical future users.

Build for the current mission.
